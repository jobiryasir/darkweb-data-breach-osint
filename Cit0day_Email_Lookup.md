This document outlines the methodology for researching credentials potentially compromised in the Cit0day breach (a compilation of 23,000+ breaches from 2020).

---

### ⚠️ Security Warning
**Do not use untrusted "Cit0day Search" sites.** Many malicious actors host fake lookup portals to collect your email address and password attempts. **Only use reputable, established security platforms** to verify if your data appears in this legacy compilation.

### 🔍 Investigative Methodology

1. **Primary Check:** Perform a standard lookup on **Have I Been Pwned**, **MyOSINT Breach**, or **BreachDirectory**. If an email appears in a breach dated around 2020, it is highly likely included in the Cit0day compilation.
2. **Contextual Analysis:** If confirmed, identify the specific services associated with the leak to map out password reuse patterns.
3. **Data Scrubbing:** Since Cit0day is a massive historical compilation, focus on changing passwords for all services associated with the leaked email address, not just one.

---

### 📝 Researcher's Checklist for Legacy Breaches

- [ ] Am I using a clean, non-PII investigative email?
- [ ] Have I verified the source is reputable before entering identifiers?
- [ ] Am I documenting the breach timeline (2020) for reference?
- [ ] Have I updated my security posture for all services identified in the lookup?
